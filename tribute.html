A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Tribute to Missile man of India.

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam

Indian scientist and honorable 11th president (2002-2007) of India stepped to this world on october 15,1931. He played a leading role in devoloping missile and nuclear weapon projects, which rewarded him with a nickname "The Missile Man".

Early Life

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born into a Muslim family on October 15, 1931, on the island of Dhanushkodi off the southeastern coast of India. He was a bright student who showed promise in science and mathematics. He attended St. Joseph's College and went on to earn a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.

Life Timeline

1931
Legend was born

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 to a Tamil Muslim family in the pilgrimage centre of Rameswaram on Pamban Island, then in the Madras Presidency and now in the State of Tamil Nadu.

Joined Madras Institute of Technology

Kalam moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology. He narrowly missed achieving his dream of becoming a fighter pilot, as he placed ninth in qualifiers, and only eight positions were available in the IAF.

1955
1960
Joined DRDO

After graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960, Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (by Press Information Bureau, Government of India) as a scientist after becoming a member of the Defence Research & Development Service (DRDS). He started his career by designing a small hovercraft, but remained unconvinced by his choice of a job at DRDO. Kalam was also part of the INCOSPAR committee working under Vikram Sarabhai, the renowned space scientist.

Visit to NASA

In 1963 to 1964, he visited NASA's Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia; Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland; and Wallops Flight Facility.

1963
1965
Started independent project

Kalam had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO in 1965.

Joined ISRO

In 1969, Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980

1969
1970
PSLV and SLV-III success

Between the 1970s and 1990s, Kalam made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful.

Honored with Padma Bhushan

The Government of India honoured him with the Padma Bhushan in 1981

1981
1990
Honored with Padma Vibhushan

The Government of India honoured him with the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 for his work with ISRO and DRDO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.

Appointed India’s Chief Scientific Adviser

Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation from July 1992 to December 1999.

1992
1997
Honoured with the Bharat Ratna

Kalam is awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.

Spearheads the Pokhran mission

India conducts the Pokhran II nuclear tests with Kalam being one of the forces behind its success.

1998
1999
Serves as India’s Chief Scientific Adviser

From 1999 till 2001, he served as Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India.

Elected as India's 11th President

Kalam is elected the 11th President of India succeeding KR Narayanan. He won the 2002 presidential election with an electoral vote of 922,884. During his five years in office, he was fondly called ‘The People’s President'

2002
2012
Launches 'What Can I Give Movement'

Kalam launches the ‘What Can I Give Movement’, a programme for the Indian youth aimed at defeating corruption.

Received Von Braun Award

he was the recipient of the Von Braun Award from the National Space Society "to recognize excellence in the management and leadership of a space-related project". Following his death, Kalam received numerous tributes.

2013
2015
Passes away in Shillong, Meghalaya

Kalam passes away aged 83, after collapsing because of a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.

Famous books written by him

INDIA 2020

India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium is a book, written by late former President of India Dr. A P J Abdul Kalam and Dr. Y S Rajan.

WINGS OF FIRE

Wings of Fire: An Autobiography of A P J Abdul Kalam (1999), former President of India. It was written by Dr. Kalam and Arun Tiwari.

IGNITED MINDS

Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India 2020 is a book written by A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, who was the President of India from 2002 to 2007. Ignited Minds is a logical step forward from Dr. Kalam's earlier book, India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium. This book consists of many inspirational messages by A P J Abdul Kalam.

Target 3 Billion

Target 3 Billion is a book by the former President of India, A. P. J. Abdul Kalam, and Srijan Pal Singh. The book highlights the issues prevailing in rural India and suggests measures to improve standards of living. It focuses on the inclusive development project called PURA (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas).
